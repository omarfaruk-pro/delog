Brokerage Services

Customs brokerage services offer a full range of documentation collection. An incorrectly filled out customs declaration when receiving cargo can cause serious problems. We will facilitate your relationship with customs, avoid fines and inconvenience caused by cargo delays.

What Does a Freight Broker Do?

We have automated 85% of the process.

Documentation Handling

The freight process begins with thorough documentation. The broker collects all necessary details about your shipment, such as weight, dimensions, type of cargo, and specific delivery instructions. They also ensure that any required documentation for handling special goods (such as hazardous materials or oversized items) is prepared, ensuring smooth transit and regulatory compliance.

Loading Coordination

Freight brokers coordinate the loading of cargo, ensuring that goods are properly secured and handled. They connect with carriers to confirm the loading schedule and work closely with drivers to ensure timely arrivals. Once the cargo is loaded, the carrier signs the bill of lading, assuming responsibility for the goods in transit.

In-Transit Monitoring

While your goods are in transit, the broker monitors the shipment’s progress and provides real-time updates. They ensure that everything is on track and communicate any potential issues, so you stay informed throughout the process. This visibility helps reduce uncertainty and ensures smooth operations from start to finish.

Delivery and Unloading

When the goods reach their destination, the consignee signs the bill of lading to confirm receipt. After unloading, the carrier completes the necessary paperwork, and the broker ensures that all documents are finalized and sent to the shipper for processing.

Invoicing and Payment

The final step is invoicing. Once the delivery is complete, the freight broker issues an invoice to the shipper, who then processes the payment. This ensures a clear and streamlined financial transaction, closing out the shipment process.
